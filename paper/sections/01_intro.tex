\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

REST APIs carry most of the traffic between modern software components, and testing them
well remains expensive and largely manual. The arrival of LLMs that can read an OpenAPI
specification and emit executable tests is therefore commercially significant, and the
research response has been rapid: RESTGPT~\cite{restgpt} extracts input-constraint rules at
97\% precision, AutoRestTest~\cite{autoresttest} reports 42 server errors against
EvoMaster's 20, LlamaRestTest~\cite{llamaresttest} reports 204 faults against 130, and
RESTifAI~\cite{restifai} reaches 95.5\% operation coverage.

Read carefully, those numbers answer a narrower question than they appear to. Each is a
count of \emph{what a suite found on a live system}, not a fraction of \emph{what was there
to find}. Without a known fault population, ``42 versus 20'' cannot distinguish a suite that
found 42 of 50 available faults from one that found 42 of 500. For a QA lead deciding
whether to trust a generator, the missing denominator is the whole question.

\subsection{Gaps}
Our systematic review of \textbf{59} papers (2018--2026, five independent searches merged
into \texttt{team-synthesis/evidence-table-merged.md}) isolates three persistent gaps.

\textbf{GAP-C (Comparison).} Exactly one study compares a generator against a manual
suite: EvoMaster~\cite{evomaster}, which found its tool \emph{below} manual (41\% vs 82\%
statement coverage). That study predates LLMs. No paper places LLM, manual, and a mature
tool on the same APIs, so the field's central practical claim --- that LLM tests rival
human ones --- has never been tested directly.

\textbf{GAP-D (Dataset / ground truth).} Fault detection is universally reported as raw
500-error counts on live
APIs~\cite{llamaresttest,autoresttest,logiagent,notimetorest,evomaster,morest}. With no
shared pre-seeded-fault benchmark, ground-truth Recall is never computed.

\textbf{GAP-M (Metric).} Evaluation is coverage-biased. Only KAT~\cite{kat} splits
2xx/4xx and only RESTSpecIT~\cite{restspecit} reports 5xx; no work reports a per-endpoint
edge-case metric or an endpoint-type miss profile.

\subsection{This paper}
We close GAP-C and GAP-D jointly by seeding faults with standard mutation operators into
three EMB APIs, which fixes the denominator at \RQIIn{} known faults, and then running
all three arms against every mutant. We address GAP-M with a per-endpoint edge-case count
and a code-derived \emph{error-surface baseline} that enumerates, per endpoint, every
source location able to emit a non-2xx response --- a tool-independent map of what
\emph{can} fail, against which each arm's behaviour is read.

The outcome splits cleanly, and against expectation. On the metrics the literature
favours, the LLM wins comfortably: \RQIcoveragePct\% endpoint coverage and
\RQIIItotalLlm{} edge-case scenarios against manual's \RQIIItotalManual{}
($p=\RQIIIpvalue$). On the ground truth, it does not win at all: Recall
\RQIIrecallLlm{} against EvoMaster's \RQIIrecallEvo{}. The LLM writes roughly
\RQIIItotalLlm{} scenarios and detects fewer faults than a search-based tool from 2019.

That dissociation is not noise, and it is not a scoring artefact. Section~\ref{sec:discussion}
traces it to oracle \emph{kind}: the LLM's spec-derived status-code assertions accept any
response within a plausible class and so cannot see a fault that changes a computed value
while leaving the status untouched --- which is what most of our mutants do. EvoMaster's
recorded-value regression oracles see precisely those. The mechanism explains why a suite
can be simultaneously more thorough by every published metric and less effective at the
job.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{The first three-way comparison} of LLM, manual, and EvoMaster test
        generation on identical APIs under a blind protocol (GAP-C).
  \item \textbf{A pre-seeded-fault benchmark} of \RQIIn{} catalogued mutants over
        \RQIopsTotal{} operations, enabling true fault-detection Recall rather than
        live-API error counts (GAP-D).
  \item \textbf{A per-endpoint edge-case metric plus a code-derived error-surface
        baseline}, separating scenarios \emph{produced} from faults \emph{detected} (GAP-M).
  \item \textbf{An oracle-kind explanation} of why the two diverge, with the practical
        consequence that scenario-count metrics are misleading when reported alone.
  \item \textbf{A fully reproducible pipeline}: every number in this paper is generated
        from committed raw data by a script, not transcribed (Section~\ref{sec:method-repro}).
\end{enumerate}
